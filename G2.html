What is Design Thinking?

Design Thinking is a non-linear process that focuses on defining and solving a problem that is plaguing a user.
It consists of 5 phases, not necessarily in a strict order, that help the designer in creating what the problem
needs and how it should be solved. It also involves “thinking outside the box” as the easiest solutions are
sometimes hard to come by due to restrictions in one’s profession or perspective.

The 5 phases are: Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Testing. These are usually done in that order however
there are instances that you can go to previous steps in order to review the product in order to refine the method
or come up with more efficient solutions to the problem.

One theme is that is always referenced is the ability to “think outside the box”. This is because some problems
are solved through unusual means that have the most efficient method. A story referenced is the truck that got
stuck under a bridge when it didn’t have enough clearance. Many experts tried various solutions that yielded
the truck either being disassembled or chipping the bridge. One boy passing by suggested to deflate the tires
and let the truck move on its own. Even when experts are in the area, it's always good to be open to others or
through sources.
