front-end dev

Information Architecture seems to be a vital part in UI design. The topic covered on the slide focuses on Web Design aspect. It taught me the principles of good web design and how it gave me perspective on what the users find first. Just like an actual library, the website should show where the user is where, and where can they go.


It also describes the idea of essential information being at the forefront, but also for detailed information being the first thing that the user would look for. Such as copy and pasting the link of the information they need to the search engine and finding what they are looking for.


Some games don’t even let the player look for the information they need on the game, though they would often put compromises. One issue we often have to deal with is with MMOs. Because of the RPG aspect, the player has to view all the necessary numbers and stats that their player or their enemy must show, the UI is often cluttered with information just at the UI. The menu or character screen would show all the concrete numbers that make up the simple turn-based combat.


An example would be from WoW, where the bottom of the screen shows the icons of the character’s abilities, but not their effects. If they do want a more detailed information, they would open the character screen from the menu and look at their ability list. The designers put the idea of just hovering the mouse on the ability icon bare essential information of the player needs to know.
