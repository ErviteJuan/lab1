Looking at what I had learned from USERDES,I learned the definition of UX and UI design and how these two come together, the type of clients I will encounter, the processes of creating a user-friendly product, and how I can integrate both to cater for the potential customers and clients.


The steps in creating an effective user interface for software hinges on the clientâ€™s needs. This was hammered on when researching what various people need, learning what their pain points are, and how it can be prevented when implementing the overall design. One way was creating scenarios like Personas which represent the possible types of people you encounter and give an idea on what the user interface should feel like.


One experience that could provide insight on what I should and should not do was the User Inyerface exercise which focused on creating an anti-user interface that was designed to frustrate the user. I was interested on how they manage to create the format and how they twisted the common UI design to fool the user. Adding steps when inputting your username, mislabeling the pictures, etc. It was an interesting experience and give the idea what I should not do and how can I prevent it when I design my own software.
